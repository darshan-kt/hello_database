INSURANCE PRE-AUTHORIZATION POLICY

SCOPE
This policy applies to all elective inpatient admissions, non-emergency
surgical procedures, advanced imaging (MRI, CT, PET), and any
treatment exceeding the hospital's pre-authorization cost threshold of
$1,500.

WHEN PRE-AUTHORIZATION IS REQUIRED
- Elective (non-emergency) inpatient admission
- Elective surgery, including day-surgery procedures above the
  threshold
- MRI, CT, and PET imaging (except when ordered from the Emergency
  Department for an acute presentation)
- Durable medical equipment above $500
- Out-of-network specialist referrals

WHEN PRE-AUTHORIZATION IS NOT REQUIRED
- Emergency Department treatment for a condition that reasonably
  appears to be an emergency
- Inpatient admission directly from the Emergency Department for an
  emergent condition
- Routine outpatient office visits with an in-network provider

PROCESS
1. The ordering clinician's office submits the pre-authorization
   request through the insurance portal at least 5 business days
   before the scheduled service (2 business days for urgent but
   non-emergency cases).
2. Include clinical justification, relevant diagnostic codes, and prior
   conservative treatment attempted, where applicable.
3. The insurer responds within 3 business days for standard requests,
   or 24 hours for urgent requests.
4. If denied, the ordering clinician may submit a peer-to-peer review
   request within 10 business days, or file a formal appeal.

BILLING IMPLICATIONS
Services requiring pre-authorization that are performed without an
approved authorization on file may be denied by the insurer and become
the patient's financial responsibility, subject to the hospital's
financial hardship policy. Registration staff must verify an active
authorization is on file before an elective procedure is scheduled.

PATIENT COMMUNICATION
Patients must be informed if a required pre-authorization is pending
or has been denied, and given the estimated out-of-pocket cost, before
an elective service is rendered.
