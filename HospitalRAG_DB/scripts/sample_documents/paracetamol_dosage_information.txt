DRUG INFORMATION SHEET: PARACETAMOL (ACETAMINOPHEN)

Class: Analgesic / Antipyretic

INDICATIONS
Mild to moderate pain relief; fever reduction. First-line analgesic for
most patients, including those for whom NSAIDs are contraindicated
(e.g. peptic ulcer disease, dengue fever, anticoagulant use).

ADULT DOSAGE
500-1000 mg orally every 4-6 hours as needed.
Maximum 4000 mg (4 g) per 24 hours for short-term use in healthy
adults. For patients with chronic alcohol use, hepatic impairment, or
malnourishment, reduce the maximum daily dose to 2000-3000 mg.

PEDIATRIC DOSAGE
10-15 mg/kg per dose orally every 4-6 hours as needed.
Maximum 5 doses (75 mg/kg) in 24 hours. Always dose pediatric
paracetamol by weight, not by age alone.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
- Severe hepatic impairment or active liver failure
- Known hypersensitivity to paracetamol

WARNINGS
Paracetamol overdose is a leading cause of acute liver failure.
Patients must be counseled to check all combination cold/flu products
for paracetamol content to avoid inadvertent double-dosing. Chronic
alcohol use increases hepatotoxicity risk even at standard doses.

DRUG INTERACTIONS
- Warfarin: chronic paracetamol use (regular dosing over several days)
  can potentiate the INR-raising effect of warfarin; monitor INR more
  closely if used together for more than 2-3 days.
- Isoniazid and other CYP2E1 inducers increase the risk of
  hepatotoxicity.

OVERDOSE MANAGEMENT
Acetylcysteine (NAC) is the antidote, most effective within 8 hours of
ingestion but still indicated later. Obtain a serum paracetamol level
at 4 hours post-ingestion (or as soon as possible after) and plot
against the Rumack-Matthew nomogram to determine treatment need.

STORAGE
Store at room temperature, away from moisture. Keep out of reach of
children -- pediatric liquid formulations are a common source of
accidental overdose.
